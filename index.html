1900's

W.E.B. Du Bois

American sociologist, socialist, historian, civil rights activist, Pan-Africanist, author, writer and editor

1868-1963

At a young age she decided she wanted to be a graphic designer. In 1958, she began her career in New York City. She finished school at Cooper Union in 1967. "I've never been afraid of anything or anybody in my life," she said. Early on, she new it was going to be a task to win the respect as a design and a woman during this time.

In 1970 she organized an exhibit at Rhode Island School of Design. It showcased forty-nine young black graphic designers, Black Artist in Graphic Communication.
